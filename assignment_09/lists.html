Top 10 Places I've Traveled

  1. Scotland

    From June to September of 2013, Brian and I lived in Glasgow, Scotland. We were there because Brian got a grant from the Whitaker Foundation so that he could do research at Strathclyde University. While we were there we traveled all around Scotland and to other countries.

    Highlights from Scotland:

    • Glasgow's West End
    • Edinburgh Castle
    • Glencoe
    • Eilean Donan Castle
    • St. Andrew's Old Course

    Tip: If you plan on seeing more than one castle, join the Scotland Historical Society and you will get a deal on tickets.

  2. Amsterdam

    While Brian and I were in Scotland, we decided to spontaneously take a weekend trip to Amsterdam, The Netherlands. We had never considered visiting Amsterdam, but we found really inexpensive tickets and decided to go. It ended up being one of my favorite trips.

    Highlights from Amsterdam:

    • The Canals
    • Ann Frank House
    • Vlaams Friteshuis Vleminckx i.e. Dutch French Fries
    • Tulips
    • Museums

    Tip: Use AirBnb to book a place to stay!

  3. Dublin

    Brian and I were lucky enough to take a trip to Dublin, Ireland with my family last summer. My mom's ancestors are Irish so it has always been a dream of her's to visit there. We were there for several days and had a great time.

    Highlights from Dublin:

    • Temple Bar District
    • Kilmainham Jaol
    • Day Trip to Wicklow Mountains
    • Musical Pub Crawl
    • Fish n' Chips

    Tip: Buy a day pass for a hop-on-hop-off tour bus. Cheap way to get around to all the major sights!

  4. Paris

    I met up with a girlfriend of mine for a short trip to Paris. Paris had never been at the top of my list for some reason, but I am so glad I went! So many beautiful things to see and do.

    Highlights from Paris:

    • The Lourve
    • Notre Dame
    • Eiffle Tower
    • Palace of Versailles
    • Falaffels in the Jewish District

    Tip: Travel with a friend who is fluent in French! It made everything so much easier.

  5. London

    I got to visit London twice while we lived in Scotland. Brian and I took an overnight bus to get there and then tried to see and do as many things as we could do in two days. The second time, I went with my parents just for one night on our way to Bath, UK.

    Highlights from London:

    • Westminster Abbey
    • Tower of London
    • British Library
    • Hyde Park
    • Churchill War Rooms

    Tip: Download the Rick Steves Tour Guide app! Free walking tours. Just put in your earphones and he tells you where to go and all about what you are seeing!

  6. Cinque Terre

    Near the end of our stay in Scotland, Brian had a conference in Genoa, Italy. So I tagged along and we got to hike along the Cinque Terre the day before the conference started. It is a portion of the Italian Riviera composed of five villiages that sit on the coast and overlook the sea. Paths, trains and boats connect the villages, and cars cannot reach them from the outside.

    Highlights from Cinque Terre:

    • The beautiful views
    • The beaches
    • Lunch in Vernazza
    • Gnocci and pesto are local specialites
    • Trains also connect the cities if you get tired

    Tip: Wear good tennis shoes and we ready for a hike! It was very tiring and at times a little dangerous due to a very narrow path and steep cliffs, but totally worth it!

  7. San Francisco, California

    A few years ago, just after Brian and I got married he had a conference in San Francisco, California so I got to tag along and sightsee.

    Highlights from San Francisco:

    • Lombard Street
    • Coit Tower
    • Golden Gate Bridge
    • Embarcadero Ferry Building
    • Riding the Trolly

    Tip: Be ready for lots of hills!

  8. Washington, D.C.

    For my 13th birthday, my dad took me on a father-daughter trip to D.C. and then I also went and visited a friend their last spring.

    Highlights from Washington, D.C.:

    • Lincoln Memorial
    • Touring the Capitol
    • White House
    • We, The Pizza (Capitol Hill)
    • Georgetown

    Tip: Go in the spring when the cherry blossoms are in bloom.

  9. Argentina

    The summer between my junior and senior year of college I studied aborad in Buenos Aires, Argentina.

    Highlights from Argentina:

    • Iguazo Falls
    • Plaza San Martin
    • Tango Lessons
    • The Nightlife
    • Recoleta

    Tip: Try a media luna!

  10. St. John

    In high school, my family took a vacation to St. John, one of the U.S. Virgin Islands. It was paradise!

    Highlights from St. John:

    • Parasailing
    • Private Beach
    • Its a small island so you can easily drive all the way around in one day
    • Snorkeling
    • Fresh Sea Food

    Tip: Wear sunscreen!

Top 10 Favorite TV Shows

  1. Parks and Rec

    Summary: An American comedy television series about the absurd antics of an Indiana town's public officials as they pursue sundry projects to make their city a better place.

    Cast: Amy Poehler, Aziz Ansari, Nick Offerman, Aubrey Plaza, Chris Pratt, Jim O'Heir, Retta, Rashida Jones, Adam Scott, and Rob Lowe

    Number of Seasons: 7

    Originally Broadcast On: NBC

  2. Homeland

    Summary: A political thriller tabout a Central Intelligence Agency officer with bipolar disorder, that has come to believe that a United States Marine Corps Scout Sniper, who was held captive by al-Qaeda as a prisoner of war, was "turned" by the enemy and now threatens the United States.

    Originally Broadcast On: Showtime

  3. House of Cards

    Summary: A political drama set in present-day Washington, D.C.It is the story of Frank Underwood, a Democrat from South Carolina's 5th Congressional District and House Majority Whip who teams up with his wife to exact his revenge on those who betrayed him.

    Originally Broadcast On: Netflix

  4. Modern Family

    Summary: An American television sitcom that is presented in mockumentary style (the fictional characters frequently talk directly into the camera). It is the story of Jay Pritchett, his second wife, his stepson, and their infant son; and his two adult children and their families.

    Originally Broadcast On: ABC

  5. Mad Men

    Summary: A period drama series set in the 1960s, at an advertising agency on Madison Avenue in New York City. The phrase "Mad men" was a slang term coined in the 1950s by advertisers working on Madison Avenue to refer to themselves. The focal point of the series is Don Draper, creative director at Sterling Cooper and a founding partner at Sterling Cooper Draper Pryce, and the people in his life, both in and out of the office.

    Originally Broadcast On: A&E

  6. Downton Abbey

    Summary: A British period drama television series set in the fictional Yorkshire country estate of Downton Abbey. It depicts the lives of the aristocratic Crawley family and their servants in the post-Edwardian era with the great events in history having an effect on their lives and on the British social hierarchy.

    Originally Broadcast On: PBS

  7. Castle

    Summary: A crime drama television series that traces the lives of Richard Castle (Nathan Fillion), a best-selling mystery novelist, and Kate Beckett (Stana Katic), an NYPD homicide detective, as they solve various unusual crimes in New York City.

    Originally Broadcast On: ABC

  8. Psych

    Summary: A detective comedy-drama television series about a young crime consultant for the Santa Barbara Police Department whose "heightened observational skills" and impressive detective instincts allow him to convince people that he solves cases with psychic abilities.

    Originally Broadcast On: USA

  9. Friends

    Summary: An American sitcom which originally aired for ten seasons on NBC from 1994 to 2004. It revolves around a circle of friends living in Manhattan, a borough of New York City.

    Originally Broadcast On: NBC

  10. House

    Summary: An American television medical drama. The show's main character is Dr. Gregory House (Hugh Laurie), a drug-addicted, unconventional, misanthropic medical genius who leads a team of diagnosticians at the fictional Princeton–Plainsboro Teaching Hospital (PPTH) in New Jersey.

    Originally Broadcast On: Fox
